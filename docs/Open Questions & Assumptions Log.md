# Doggo – Open Questions & Assumptions Log (v1)

This document tracks **known unknowns**, assumptions, and risks in the Doggo product.
Its purpose is to make uncertainty explicit, visible, and actionable — not to resolve everything upfront.

Questions here are expected. Untracked assumptions are the real risk.

## Related docs

- Current product scope boundaries live in **MVP Scope Boundaries** (`docs/MVP Scope Boundaries.md`).
- Trust progression and signal rules live in **Trust Model** (`docs/Trust Model.md`).

---

## How to Use This Document

- Add questions as soon as they arise
- Do not rush to resolve them
- Mark assumptions clearly
- Revisit this log at each major product decision

---

## 1. Trust & Behavior Assumptions

**Assumption:**
Dog owners in Prague are willing to build trust through repeated proximity and shared routines.

**Open Questions:**

- Will users actually recognize and care about "familiar faces" digitally?
- How many in-person interactions are needed before trust feels real?
- Do users prefer explicit confirmation ("we met") or implicit signals?

---

## 2. Meetups & Real-World Interaction

**Assumption:**
Low-pressure walk meetups are socially acceptable and desirable.

**Open Questions:**

- Should meetups be owner-created or platform-suggested?
- What is the minimum structure needed to avoid awkwardness?
- How visible should meetups be to non-participants?

---

## 3. Safety & Liability

**Assumption:**
Lightweight safeguards are sufficient in early stages.

**Open Questions:**

- What liability does the platform assume during meetups?
- Are disclaimers enough, or are additional safeguards expected?
- What incidents require immediate trust regression?

---

## 4. Role Progression

**Assumption:**
Users are comfortable evolving from owner -> helper -> provider gradually.

**Open Questions:**

- At what point does "helping" feel like a job?
- What signals justify unlocking helper or provider status?
- How reversible should role progression be?

---

## 5. Monetization Timing

**Assumption:**
Trust must exist before monetization is introduced.

**Open Questions:**

- What user behavior indicates readiness for paid interactions?
- Should payment be peer-to-peer first or platform-mediated?
- Does monetization reduce trust if introduced too early?

---

## 6. Cultural & Local Norms (Prague)

**Assumption:**
Prague dog culture values routine, parks, and informal socialization.

**Open Questions:**

- Are there existing informal dog-owner networks we're replicating?
- What expectations exist around verification and identity?
- Are there local sensitivities around data visibility or privacy?

---

## 7. Platform Expectations

**Assumption:**
Users will accept fewer features in exchange for higher trust.

**Open Questions:**

- Will users compare Doggo directly to Rover-like platforms?
- What missing feature would immediately cause drop-off?
- How much explanation does the trust model require?

---

## 8. Agent & Build Strategy

**Assumption:**
Agent-assisted building will accelerate execution without distorting intent.

**Open Questions:**

- Which decisions must remain human-only?
- What guardrails do agents need most?
- How often should agent output be reviewed or discarded?

---

## Review Cadence

This document should be reviewed:

- After early user conversations
- Before expanding scope
- Before introducing monetization

Questions can move from **Open** -> **Partially Answered** -> **Resolved**, but should never be deleted without reason.
