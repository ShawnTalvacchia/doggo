# Doggo – User Archetypes (v1)

These archetypes describe **behavioral patterns**, not demographics.  
They exist to help reason about trust, motivation, and product decisions — especially for agents generating flows, copy, and features.

---

## 1. The Routine Owner

**Who they are**  
A consistent dog owner with regular walking times and familiar routes (same parks, same streets, same hours).

**Primary goals**

- Keep their dog healthy and socialized
- Maintain predictable routines
- Avoid unnecessary hassle

**Primary fears**

- Letting a stranger disrupt their routine
- Unsafe or chaotic interactions
- Over-commitment

**Trust threshold**

- Moderate  
  Trust increases through repeated visibility and shared routines, not credentials.

**What success feels like**

- Their dog has familiar faces (human and canine)
- They recognize people before needing help
- Help feels available without being transactional

---

## 2. The Occasional-Need Owner

**Who they are**  
An owner who is usually self-sufficient but occasionally needs help (travel, long workdays, emergencies).

**Primary goals**

- Find reliable help when needed
- Minimize stress and risk
- Feel confident leaving their dog with someone else

**Primary fears**

- Making the wrong choice under pressure
- Hidden risks or misrepresentation
- Feeling judged for needing help

**Trust threshold**

- High  
  Needs clear signals of reliability and familiarity before acting.

**What success feels like**

- Having a short list of “known” people
- Feeling calm when plans change
- Trusting the platform to reduce uncertainty

---

## 3. The Helper-Curious Owner

**Who they are**  
An owner who enjoys dogs and would help others occasionally, but does not see themselves as a professional provider.

**Primary goals**

- Be useful without obligation
- Help within their comfort zone
- Build social connections naturally

**Primary fears**

- Being treated like a service worker
- Hidden expectations or pressure
- Liability or awkward social dynamics

**Trust threshold**

- Low to moderate  
  They trust through personal interaction more than platform guarantees.

**What success feels like**

- Helping feels casual and appreciated
- Boundaries are clear and respected
- Participation remains optional

---

## 4. The Aspiring Provider

**Who they are**  
Someone interested in offering dog-related services more regularly, possibly as side income.

**Primary goals**

- Build credibility over time
- Earn trust without being a stranger
- Transition gradually into paid help

**Primary fears**

- Being lumped in with untrusted providers
- Over-regulation too early
- Difficulty standing out fairly

**Trust threshold**

- Moderate  
  They trust the platform if progression feels fair and transparent.

**What success feels like**

- Clear steps to unlock more responsibility
- Trust signals reflect real behavior
- Income grows alongside reputation

---

## 5. The Established Provider (Later Stage)

**Who they are**  
A professional or semi-professional dog walker/sitter.

**Primary goals**

- Consistent work
- Clear expectations
- Reputation protection

**Primary fears**

- Being undercut by unqualified helpers
- Platform instability
- Unclear rules or enforcement

**Trust threshold**

- Platform-based  
  They trust systems, policies, and structure.

**What success feels like**

- Predictable demand
- Respect for professionalism
- Fair visibility and matching

---

## Archetype Notes

- All users **start as owners**
- Movement between archetypes is expected and encouraged
- Trust is built through **participation and proximity**, not labels
- The product should never force a user to adopt an archetype
