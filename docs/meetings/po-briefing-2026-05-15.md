# Doggo — Strategy Briefing: Walker-Trainer & Shelter Angles

**For:** Alyssa
**From:** Shawn
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Purpose:** Two strategy threads worth exploring before the demo locks. Surface what's worth validating and possible positioning angles.

---

## Where we are

The main features and surfaces are built; we're now shaping the demo walkthrough for user testing. In the process, a couple of strategic threads have surfaced that are worth sitting with — they could affect how we structure the demo and what we set out to validate with it.

---

## 1. The walker-trainer hybrid angle

**Premise.** Most paid dog people in Prague aren't just walkers OR just trainers — they're hybrids. The Tier 1 professionals from our prior competitive research (Pawz, Rocky & Skipper, Dogitory) bundle walking, training, and often sitting. The dominant solo-trainer population — 80–90% of the market per the same research — operates mobile, often in parks, often blending instruction with exercise. The walker-trainer isn't a niche; it's the most common pro shape in the market.

**Why this matters for cold-start.** Organic owner-hosted community walks aren't a behavior most people start on their own. Hosting a regular public meet is real work without an obvious payoff — owners' incentives (their dog's social life, friendships) are real but weak. Walker-trainer hybrids have a much stronger incentive stack: every walk is demonstration, lead-gen, content, and retention all at once. They're paid to do the thing the platform needs.

**Honest costs of leaning into this.**

- Some rewriting in how we frame and pitch — not a strategy change, but the story to owners, pros, and observers gets sharpened.
- More exploration on positioning — are there features we'd want to add or sharpen that better suit this angle? Recurring-meet portfolio tools, clearer meet → booking conversion surfaces, lead-gen visibility from a meet a pro hosts. Most likely modest, but worth a pass.
- Some additional time structuring the demo so the walker-trainer thread reads clearly alongside the community thread, rather than feeling like a sidebar.

On the upside, the core features are already built. Meets, recurring cadence, Carer profile, trust badges, post-meet review — all of it serves this user. The investment is in framing and demo work, not new product surface.

**What this changes about cold-start.** One idea for seeding early activity has been to partner with a few trainers. This expands that thinking — instead of treating trainers as a special seed group, we recruit the more common walker-trainer pro and lean into a pattern that already fits their work: turning park time into demonstration and lead-gen at once. Same engine, better-aimed at the shape of pros that actually exist in this market.

## 2. The shelter angle — what the research actually shows

You mentioned hearing that some shelters don't allow people to just walk in off the street and walk a shelter dog. I had Claude do some research, and it looks like that's likely not the case for many of the organizations. Útulek Troja is a municipal shelter (run by Praha 8) and doesn't allow public walking. But most other Prague shelters run active public walking programs.

Dogpoint, Voříškov, Útulek Liběň, Bedfor Pet, Cool Critters, Dog Planet, Projekt HOPE, Cricksy Dog, Psí domov Řepnice — all confirmed via their own websites and the Suri.cz overview. The onboarding pattern is consistent across them:

1. Online reservation system (already digital at most)
2. First-time visit with brief in-person intro by shelter staff
3. Sign a waiver acknowledging the rules
4. Dog assigned by staff based on volunteer's experience, not freely chosen
5. Typical 2-hour time slot with a 30-minute minimum walk
6. Standard rules — no off-leash, avoid other dogs, 18+, no walks in extreme weather

The Czech-language guide on Suri.cz explicitly frames shelter walking as "one of the most accessible forms of volunteering." This isn't a closed-gate situation across the city; it's an onboarding step that varies per shelter.

**The more interesting reframe.** Multiple shelters already use online booking infrastructure. Doggo isn't proposing a behavior that doesn't exist — it's proposing **aggregation and coordination over fragmented per-shelter systems.** Bilingual (Czech/English) coordination is itself valuable: most shelters operate Czech-first while the expat dog community is English-first, and that gap is real.

**Namespace check.** A Czech app called Petsy.eu exists, but it's a petsitter platform (Rover-shape), not a shelter-walking aggregator. The opening exists.

**Implication for the playbook.** The shelter angle gets significantly more credible. Útulek Troja isn't the right partner because they don't run a walking program, but two or three others are realistic — and may actively want a better coordination layer.

## 3. Potential framing: community + marketplace, balanced

Doggo as both — a genuine community where dog people connect AND a marketplace where trainers, walkers, sitters, and groomers find clients in that active base. The two reinforce each other rather than compete, because **the same person is both customer and community member.** A walker-trainer hybrid is also an owner with their own dog. An owner booking care is also someone who attends meets. The community → trust → care funnel flows in both directions: care relationships create content and visibility, which feeds the community, which generates trust signals, which lowers friction for the next booking.

**Practical implication.** Most of this is positioning and recruitment, not features. The existing surfaces serve both worlds. What changes is the story we tell each audience:

- *To owners:* find your dog people, organize, and when you need paid care you'll find people you actually know
- *To pros:* an active community base IS your client base; meets are demonstration + lead-gen
- *To investors and observers:* community and marketplace are both load-bearing and feed each other

## 4. What we need to validate — and how

Two cheap validation moves before committing to demo redesign or doc updates.

**a) Walker-trainer conversations — 2 or 3 anchor pros.** This was already next-step #1 in the Cold-Start Playbook; what's new is the framing. Since no current model exists for "trainer-hosted public meets as a growth channel" in Prague, asking "do you currently do this?" won't validate anything. Concept-testing is the right move — paint the picture and probe:

- How do you get clients today?
- Would you host a regular free or low-cost community walk if it converted to clients?
- What would stop you?
- If 4 people came consistently, is that worth your time? What about 8?
- Where and when would you do it?

Specificity in their answers separates real engagement from polite agreement. Two out of three responding with credible specifics = thesis worth pursuing.

**b) Shelter conversations — the shelter contact you mentioned, plus one other.** Validate the aggregation thesis, not just the walking-from-the-street question:

- How do volunteer walkers currently find them — Facebook, expat groups, word-of-mouth?
- Would they value a bilingual coordination layer that handles booking, intro scheduling, and walker history across multiple shelters?
- What's their biggest pain point — recruitment, retention, vouching, no-shows?
- Would they let "Doggo-verified walkers" (people with a track record at the shelter) skip the intro for subsequent visits?

Either way, the contact is worth engaging. If their shelter runs a walking program, the questions above land directly. If not, they likely still have a view on how volunteer walking works across Prague and can point you toward other facilities worth talking to.

## 5. Demo implications

The demo is close to done and could go to testing as-is. Or we could dig into this thread a bit more and decide whether it's worth first refocusing or reshaping the demo around the walker-trainer angle. Testing sooner gets us real-user feedback faster; pausing to explore first sharpens what we're testing for. Worth deciding together.

## 6. Open questions for you

Open threads I'd love your read on before we commit to anything.

1. **Do the framings land?** The walker-trainer hybrid angle and the both-layers-balanced direction — do they feel right, or is one stronger than the other?
2. **Does the validation plan feel like enough?** A few walker-trainer conversations + your shelter contact + maybe one other shelter — would you want a different or wider read?

4. **Who could you imagine approaching for the 2-3 walker-trainer conversations?** Trainers in Vinohrady, Letná, Karlín, or Smíchov would be the priority profile. If you don't know any, that itself is useful — it shapes how cold outreach has to work.
5. **On the demo** — test as-is, or pause to explore this further first?
