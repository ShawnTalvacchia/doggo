## The synthesis (the one-paragraph read)

The Prague dog-care market is **fragmented and almost entirely transactional** — and **more crowded than our earlier research assumed.** Every player is a point solution: booking marketplaces (Hlídačky, PetBacker, plus direct operators like Pawz and Dogitory), socialization-matching apps (Hafinder, and likely others), and single-shelter walking tools (Dog Point). No one connects them. **Doggo's white space is integrating the whole funnel — community → trust → care → shelter → give-back — not being a nicer care marketplace.** That's the moat, and it's a thesis to prove, not an empty field to walk into.

## What changed from the older research

- **The "community / socialization lane is empty" claim was wrong.** Hafinder — a Czech dog-socialization app built by a 20-year dog school, ~3,600 downloads as of mid-2025 — is doing exactly the social-matching layer we thought we owned. We're not first to dog-socialization in Czech; traction is still modest, so the lane is *opened, not owned.*
- **The transactional lane is occupied, so "book a walker" is a losing position.** Hlídačky (local incumbent — badges + insurance), PetBacker (thin global aggregator, SEO-heavy), and several direct operators already cover the basic utility. The live risk is **positioning-drift** toward that lane.
- **Fluv has converged on our thesis.** Once "purely transactional," Fluv (Taiwan/Japan — *not* Prague) now leads with community + welfare + give-back to stray and adoptable animals. It's the closest conceptual proof the integrated model can work — and a doc that now needs updating.
- **Naming flag.** An existing "DogGO / Doggo" pet-services app (Turkey, not Prague) — worth a trademark / branding gut-check.
