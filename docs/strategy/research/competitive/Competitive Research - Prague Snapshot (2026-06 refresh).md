# Prague Competitive Snapshot — Refresh (June 2026)

*Working refresh of the competitive picture, prompted by finding that the existing docs (`Competitive Research - Prague Dog Care Scene.md`, last researched 2026-04-15) miss several current players — including some sitting in what we'd called Doggo's "white space." Treat this as a draft to fold into the formal competitive docs after a hands-on verification pass (noted at the end).*

---

## The headline correction

We have been saying the **community / socialization lane is empty**. It is not. There is at least one real, live Czech app doing dog-socialization matching — **Hafinder** — built by a 20-year dog school, with press coverage and modest traction. The demand thesis we thought we were first to is already being acted on locally.

That does not sink the strategy — but it changes the white-space claim from "nobody is doing community" to something narrower and more honest: **nobody is doing the *whole funnel*.** More on that below.

---

## The landscape, by lane

The market is fragmented into point solutions. Each player owns one slice; none connects them.

### Lane A — Transactional care (crowded)

| Player | What it is | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hlídačky.cz** | Largest *local* care marketplace. Brno-origin (~8 yrs), direct owner↔sitter contact, verification badges, sitters auto-insured to CZK 25,000/pet | The real local incumbent for *booking*. Doc's "689 Prague sitters" figure unverified now. |
| **PetBacker** | Global aggregator (50 countries). Booking + payment protection + insurance + GPS walk tracking | "Present," not deep. Thin, expat-skewed provider pool; glossy city pages are auto-generated boilerplate. Claims ~295 Prague walking listings (their own SEO number — treat skeptically). |
| **Rover** | Global marketplace, listed on Czech Google Play | Traction in Prague unverified; likely thin. |
| **Pawsapp** | On-demand local dog-walker booking app | Traction unverified. |
| **Pawz** (est. 2012), **Dogitory** (10+ yrs), **ZOOservice**, **DogHoliday.cz**, **Dogsitting.cz** | Direct professional walking/sitting/training services (not marketplaces) | Established small operators. |

None of these do community-first discovery or shelter advocacy. All compete on "book a care service."

### Lane B — Socialization / walking-partner matching (the lane we thought was empty)

| Player | What it is | Traction |
|---|---|---|
| **Hafinder** (hafinder.cz) | "Social network for dogs." Profile your dog → algorithm matches compatible companions → organize joint walks. Built by Koira dog school (Ostrava, 20 yrs). iOS + Android | ~3,600 downloads as of ~May 2025. Real but modest. Some app-stability complaints. |
| **Happy Together** | Surfaced as "arrange walks with new friends, discover walking places" — but I could **not** verify it on a second pass | **Unverified** — possibly minor or misidentified. |
| **Ocásci** (ocasci.cz) | Announced as a "new app for dog people" | **Emerging / unverified.** |

These do community/socialization — but **no care booking, no trust→paid-care, no shelter, no give-back.** They're the social layer only.

### Lane C — Shelter dog walking (standalone)

| Player | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Dog Point** (dog-point.cz) | A Prague shelter running its *own* walk-reservation app for volunteers |

Proves shelter-walk demand is real and already partly digitized — but it's a single shelter's tool, not connected to any wider community or care network.

### Conceptual reference (not in Prague) — worth watching

**Fluv** (Taiwan/Japan/HK) is still live (Jan 2026 app update) and now describes itself as *"a community platform dedicated to pet welfare — share, connect, and give back to stray and adoptable animals,"* plus sitter matching. That is strikingly close to **Doggo's own community + shelter + give-back thesis.** It's not a Prague competitor, but it's the closest *conceptual* proof that the integrated model can work — and a possible future entrant. (Our existing `Competitive Research - Fluv.md` characterizes Fluv as purely transactional with "no community layer." That characterization is now **stale** — Fluv has moved toward our space.)

## Where Doggo's white space actually is

Not "community is empty." The honest, defensible claim:

> **Every Prague player is a point solution. Nobody integrates the full funnel — socialization/community → earned trust → booked care → shelter advocacy → give-back — into one trust engine.**

- Hlídačky / PetBacker / Pawsapp / direct operators = **care booking**
- Hafinder = **socialization matching**
- Dog Point = **shelter walking**

Doggo's bet is that the *connected* funnel beats a fragmented set of single-purpose tools — that trust built in the community layer is what makes the care layer convert and the shelter layer credible. That's a real and differentiated position.

But state it as what it is: **integration-as-moat is a thesis, not an empty field.** Two honest risks:

1. **Point solutions may be "good enough."** An owner can use Hafinder for dog friends and Hlídačky to book a sitter. Doggo has to make the *connection between them* valuable enough that one integrated product beats two adequate ones.
2. **Hafinder is socialization-native and credible** (a 20-year dog school built it). We are not first to "dog socialization in Czech," and that's the layer closest to our heart. Worth studying it closely — and worth noting its traction is still modest (~3,600 downloads), so the lane is opened, not owned.

## Naming flag

There is a **"DogGO" / "Doggo" pet-services app** already (a Turkish on-demand walker platform, ~15,000 walkers in Istanbul/Ankara/İzmir — not in Prague, so not a market competitor). But the name collision is real and worth a branding/trademark/SEO check before investing further in the name.

## What's stale in the existing docs

- **`Competitive Research - Prague Dog Care Scene.md`** (researched 2026-04-15): solid on Lane A and the informal/Facebook tier, but **misses Lane B entirely** (Hafinder, etc.), misses Dog Point's shelter app, and doesn't mention Rover/Wag/Pawsapp. Several specific claims (Hlídačky's "689 sitters," "HlídačkyVerify™ video verification," Pawz/Rocky&Skipper staff credentials) are unverified and should be re-checked against live sites.
