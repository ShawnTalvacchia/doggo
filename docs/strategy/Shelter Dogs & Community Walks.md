# Shelter Dogs & Community Walks

Next step is real conversations in Prague to validate the fit before any product work happens. **TL;DR.** Prague's shelter and rescue community might be a natural fit for Doggo's community model. Could give us a credibility and warmth halo we can't easily manufacture otherwise. ## What I found

I spent some time researching Prague's rescue and foster landscape. A few things stood out:

**The institutional layer is real but constrained.** 300+ registered shelters in Czech Republic. In Prague: Pes v nouzi, Útulek Liběň, BedForPet, Cool Critters, Psi bez hranic. Most are running on volunteer labor and donations, coordinating through email, phone, and Facebook.

**There's a coordination gap that maps onto what we're already building.** Útulek Liběň recruits volunteer dog walkers — they want regular people to come walk shelter dogs. Their current ask is "1x/week, 3-month minimum, weekdays 9am–5pm" — restrictive, but I suspect that's because email-based coordination forces them to filter heavily, not because they actually need that level of commitment.

**There's a Czech/expat split.** Most rescue org websites are Czech-first. The expat dog community lives in English Facebook groups. These two communities barely talk to each other. A bilingual coordination layer could be unusually valuable.

## Why this could fit Doggo

What surprised me is how cleanly this maps onto things we've already designed:

- **Cold-start seeding.** Our plan to seed with providers who use meets for client acquisition is structurally identical to seeding with shelter volunteers who use meets for adoption. Same loop, different conversion event.
- **The Casual Helper archetype already describes this person.** Someone going to the park anyway who'd happily bring a shelter dog along. They don't need to become a "fosterer" — they just turn the helper dial slightly.
- **Badges, groups, and visibility extend naturally.** No new mechanics needed.

The first thing to build wouldn't be fostering — that's high-commitment with institutional gatekeepers. It would be **shelter dog walking meets**: a meet at Stromovka where two Útulek Liběň dogs join four owner-dogs. Same product, one new "shelter dog" attendee type, partnership with one or two shelters. Adoption flows naturally — if five people walk a sweet shelter dog over six weeks, somebody adopts. That adopter is now a Doggo user with a dog they got through this community, and a strong candidate for paid services later.

## The risk worth flagging

Foster and shelter coordination is volunteer work. Our revenue model is paid care. The risk: rescue community becomes a vibrant part of the platform without contributing to the business model, or shapes the product's center of gravity if they become our most active early users.

I think it's manageable — free care builds community, community builds trust, paid care stays naturally in reach because adopters are exactly the kind of high-LTV users we want. But worth being deliberate about. The rescue layer should *reinforce* the core funnel, not substitute for it.

## What this isn't

Not for the demo. Not on the roadmap. Not a pivot. If anything, it strengthens the existing thesis.
