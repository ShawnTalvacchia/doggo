# Competitive Research: Prague Dog Care Scene

Research conducted 2026-04-15. How Prague providers build trust today, what trust badges should look like on Doggo, and the cold-start seeding strategy.

---

## How Prague Providers Build Trust Today

Prague's dog care market splits into three tiers, each with distinct trust-building patterns.

### Tier 1: Professional services (businesses)

Examples: Pawz (est. 2012), Dogitory (10+ years), Rocky & Skipper, ZOOservice (est. 2004), PremiumVet.

**How they build trust:**

- **Formal credentials.** Czech University of Life Sciences cynology degrees, Agility Club instructor certification, Czech national vocational qualification (Národní kvalifikace — Level 4, issued by Ministry of Agriculture, covers anatomy, training methods, breed knowledge, legislation). Some trainers cite international seminar attendance (Ken Ramirez, Susan Garrett).
- **Years of operation.** Longevity is a primary trust signal. "Since 2004" or "10+ years" appears prominently.
- **Named team members with bios.** Pawz names Mirka (vet background) and Líza (Master's in Animal Science). Rocky & Skipper centres on Astrid (15 years, 200+ dogs trained). Clients reference staff by name in reviews.
- **Methodology declarations.** "Force-free, positive reinforcement" is common. Pawz explicitly states no phones/headphones during walks. These philosophy statements function as trust signals.
- **Daily photo/video updates.** Universal across professional services. Dogitory sends "multiple notifications per day with reports, photos, videos and GPS." Pawz sends "SMSes and MMSes, and more photos through email." Rocky & Skipper sends daily videos.
- **Detailed testimonials.** Long, specific client reviews citing staff by name, describing transformations ("anxious dog became confident"), noting communication quality.
- **Small group sizes / 1-on-1 care.** Positioned as premium vs. kennel/pack approaches.
- **Multi-channel availability.** WhatsApp, Messenger, email, Instagram, phone. Meeting clients where they are.

**What they DON'T do:** Insurance mentions are rare. Formal background checks aren't advertised. No platform-issued badges. Trust is entirely reputation and relationship-based.

### Tier 2: Platform sitters (gig workers)

Platforms: Hlídačky.cz (689 Prague sitters, also absorbed DogInni.cz), PetBacker, TrustedHousesitters.

**How platforms build trust:**

- **HlídačkyVerify™ badge.** Video verification — sitters complete a video call to confirm identity. Displayed prominently on profiles.
- **"Booked repeatedly" badge.** Social proof that other owners trust this sitter enough to rebook.
- **"Completed first aid course" badge.** With a dedicated icon. Specialized preparation signal.
- **Review counts + ratings.** Displayed next to pricing (e.g. "(20) reviews, 400 Kč/hour").
- **Transparent pricing.** Hourly and daily rates shown upfront. No hidden fees.
- **Filtering by trust.** "Highest-rated by families" sort option. Filter by pet type, dog size, sitter languages.
- **Language capabilities.** English/Czech/other shown on profiles — critical for Prague's expat market.

### Tier 3: Informal / word-of-mouth

The Facebook group "Pet sitting, dog walking Prague" and expat community groups (Expats.cz forums) are where many Prague dog owners actually find care. Trust here is pure social proof — someone in the group vouches for a person.

**How informal trust works:**

- Personal recommendations from named individuals
- "I've used them for 2 years" type endorsements
- Photos shared in group of dogs with sitters
- Direct messaging between recommender and seeker
- No platform intermediation — trust flows person to person

**This is what Doggo digitises.** The informal tier is Doggo's natural territory.

## Trust Badges for Doggo Provider Profiles

Based on what Prague providers actually signal and what platforms use, here's a proposed badge system that feeds into Doggo's community trust model. These badges appear on provider profiles and provider cards in search results.

### Community-earned badges (primary — unique to Doggo)

These can't be faked because they're derived from platform activity.

| Badge | How it's earned | Display |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| **Community Regular** | Attended X+ meets in the last 3 months | Icon + "Active in 4 local meets" |
| **Neighbourhood Anchor** | Connected to X+ people in the same neighbourhood | Icon + "Known by 12 people in Vinohrady" |
| **Trusted by Your Network** | X of viewer's connections use this provider | Icon + "Used by 3 people you know" |
| **Repeat Clients** | X+ owners have booked 3+ times | Icon + "8 repeat clients" |
| **Care Veteran** | Completed X+ care sessions on the platform | Icon + "120+ sessions completed" |

### Credential badges (secondary — self-declared, optionally verified)

These mirror what Prague professionals already advertise. They're entered by the provider and could be verified in production.

| Badge | What it signals | Verification level |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Certified Trainer** | Národní kvalifikace, cynology degree, Agility Club cert, international certifications | Self-declared (icon differs if platform-verified) |
| **First Aid Trained** | Pet first aid course completion | Self-declared |
| **Vet Background** | Veterinary education or experience | Self-declared |
| **Force-Free Methods** | Commitment to positive reinforcement training | Self-declared (backed by reviews mentioning it) |
| **X Years Experience** | Duration of pet care experience | Self-declared |
| **Insured** | Carries professional liability insurance | Self-declared |

### Platform badges (tertiary — awarded by Doggo)

| Badge | How it's earned | Notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **Verified Identity** | Completed ID verification process | Future production feature. Lightweight — confirms real person, not a background check. |
| **Responsive** | Replies to messages within X hours consistently | Auto-calculated from messaging data |
| **Consistent** | Low cancellation rate, shows up on time | Auto-calculated from session data |

### How badges display

- **Provider cards in search:** Show top 2-3 most relevant badges as compact pills below the name/rating. Prioritize community-earned > credential > platform.
- **Provider profile hero:** Full badge row with all earned badges. Tappable for detail ("Trusted by your network" → shows which connections).
- **Booking detail:** Relevant badges surface on the Info tab to reinforce confidence during the booking.

### Design principle

Badges reinforce the trust model — they don't replace it. A provider with zero badges but Connected status and 5 shared meets is still trustworthy. Badges help when you're evaluating someone you haven't met personally yet (the Discover → Care path).

## The Cold-Start Seeding Strategy

### The problem

Doggo needs providers with trust signals to attract owners, and owners attending meets to generate trust signals for providers. Classic two-sided marketplace cold start.

### The insight (Shawn's framing)

**Seed with initial providers who use meets/groups as a client acquisition channel.** This solves both sides simultaneously:

1. **Providers get a new acquisition tool.** Running or co-hosting meets lets them meet potential clients in a natural, non-salesy context. Dog owners see the provider interact with dogs, observe their knowledge and manner, build organic familiarity. The meet IS the sales funnel.

2. **Owners get immediate value.** Even before the marketplace has critical mass, meets and groups provide social value. The care layer is a bonus, not the only reason to join.

3. **Trust signals accumulate naturally.** Every meet the provider attends/hosts generates Community Regular, Neighbourhood Anchor, and Trusted by Your Network badges organically. By the time an owner is ready to book, the provider's profile is rich with earned signals.

### How it works in practice

**Phase 1: Recruit 3-5 seed providers in target neighbourhoods**

Target: Professional dog walkers/trainers in Vinohrady, Letná, Vršovice who already have clients but want to grow. Pitch: "Run a free weekly walk-and-train meet through Doggo. You'll meet potential clients in a natural setting, and they'll see your skills firsthand."

What providers get:
- A structured way to host community events (meets feature is already built)
- Exposure to dog owners who don't know them yet
- Trust badges that accumulate as they participate
- A booking pipeline that converts community members into paying clients
- A profile that serves as a portfolio (posts, reviews, badges, connections)

What Doggo gets:
- Real content in the platform (meets, groups, posts)
- Providers with legitimate credentials and trust signals
- A test of the community → trust → care funnel with real people
- Word-of-mouth from owners who discover providers through meets

**Phase 2: Providers host meets, owners join**

The provider runs a weekly "Morning Walk & Basic Training" meet at Letná park. 8-10 dog owners show up. After the meet, the post-meet review flow fires. Owners mark the provider (and each other) as Familiar or Connected. The provider's profile gains Community Regular and Neighbourhood Anchor badges. Owners see the provider's care services on their profile.

**Phase 3: Organic conversion**

An owner who's attended 3 meets with the same provider now has: Connected status, shared meet history, mutual connections, and has personally seen the provider handle dogs. They don't need platform vetting — they have something better. They book care through Doggo.

**Phase 4: Provider refers more providers**

The seed provider tells their trainer friend: "I got 4 new weekly clients from hosting meets on Doggo." The friend signs up. Network effects begin.

### Why this works better than traditional marketplace seeding

- **No fake trust.** Every signal is real — earned through actual community participation.
- **Providers are motivated.** Meets are directly useful to them, not busy-work to populate a platform.
- **Owners get value before they need care.** Social meets have standalone value. The care marketplace is a natural extension, not a forced conversion.
- **It tests the thesis.** If providers can't convert meet attendees into clients, that's important to know before scaling.

### Risks

- **Provider time investment.** Running weekly meets is real work. Need to ensure the ROI is there within 4-6 weeks.
- **Provider-as-host bias.** If every meet is hosted by a provider, it could feel like a sales event. Mix in owner-hosted and park-group meets to keep it organic.
- **Small market test.** 3-5 providers in 3 neighbourhoods is enough to test the loop, but not enough for a real marketplace. This is about validating the funnel, not scaling it.

## How Everything Feeds Together

```
Prague providers already build trust through:
  credentials, experience, daily updates, testimonials, word-of-mouth
                            ↓
Doggo captures and displays these as:
  credential badges (self-declared) + community-earned badges (platform data)
                            ↓
Cold-start seeding generates community badges:
  providers host meets → attend events → build connections → earn trust signals
                            ↓
Owners see rich provider profiles:
  "Certified trainer, known by 8 people in your area, 3 of your connections use them"
                            ↓
Trust gap is bridged:
  community trust (meets, connections) + credential trust (badges) + social proof (reviews, repeat clients)
                            ↓
Booking happens:
  owner confident enough to book from either path:
    - attended meets together (organic) 
    - discovered in search with strong trust signals (direct)
```
