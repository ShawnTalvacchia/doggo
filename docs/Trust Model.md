# Doggo – Trust Model / Trust Ladder (v1)

This document defines how **trust is built, signaled, and unlocked** in Doggo.  
Trust is not binary and not assumed. It progresses through visible, real-world behaviors.

The ladder applies to all users, who begin as **Owners**.

---

## Design Principles (Recap)

- Trust is earned through **participation**, not claims
- Real-world interaction > platform-only signals
- Trust should feel **earned, legible, and reversible**
- Advancement should never feel mandatory

---

## Trust Ladder Overview

Trust progresses through **observable stages**, each unlocking limited, proportional capabilities.

No stage implies obligation to move to the next.

---

## Level 0 — Presence (Default)

**How it’s earned**

- Account created
- Basic profile completed

**What it signals**

- A real person exists
- Minimal commitment

**Visible to others**

- Name, photo, dog info (if provided)
- Approximate location / usual walk areas (coarse)

**What it unlocks**

- Explore nearby owners and activity
- Observe events and meetups
- No direct requests or offers

---

## Level 1 — Familiar Face

**How it’s earned**

- Attending shared walk meetups
- Repeated proximity (same parks, similar times)
- Optional: confirming an in-person meeting

**What it signals**

- Recognizable, low-risk presence
- Social familiarity

**Visible to others**

- “Seen before” / “Met at X park”
- Shared activity indicators (non-quantified)

**What it unlocks**

- Light interactions (react, acknowledge)
- Being added to a personal “familiar” list
- Low-pressure invitations

---

## Level 2 — Known & Reliable

**How it’s earned**

- Repeated attendance
- Positive acknowledgements after interactions
- Consistent behavior over time

**What it signals**

- Predictability
- Respect for boundaries
- Social reliability

**Visible to others**

- “Known & reliable” badge (non-scored)
- Contextual notes (e.g. “regular morning walker”)

**What it unlocks**

- Direct coordination (1:1 chats)
- Informal help requests (non-paid)
- Being suggested as a trusted option

---

## Level 3 — Helper

**How it’s earned**

- Successfully helping others (walks, short care)
- Confirmed interactions by both sides
- No incidents or unresolved issues

**What it signals**

- Willingness to help
- Basic responsibility

**Visible to others**

- Helper status
- Type of help offered (walks, short sits)

**What it unlocks**

- Opt-in help offers
- Limited paid interactions (optional)
- Increased visibility for requests

---

## Level 4 — Provider

**How it’s earned**

- Identity verification (as required)
- Consistent successful help
- Optional training / checks (TBD, local norms)

**What it signals**

- Professional or semi-professional reliability
- Accountability

**Visible to others**

- Provider label
- Scope of services
- Contextual reputation (not star ratings)

**What it unlocks**

- Full paid services
- Scheduling tools
- Priority matching (later)

---

## Trust Signals (Non-Exhaustive)

Signals are **contextual, not numeric**.

Examples:

- “Shared 5 morning walks”
- “Seen regularly at Letná Park”
- “Helped with weekend care”
- “Known to X familiar owners”

Avoid:

- Public scores
- Inflated ratings
- Competitive gamification

---

## Important Constraints

- Trust **decays naturally** with inactivity
- No permanent penalties for opting out
- Users can remain at any level indefinitely
- Visibility is always **scoped and contextual**, never global by default

---
