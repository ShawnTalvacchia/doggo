# Doggo — Status & Strategic Considerations

**Date:** April 20, 2026
**Since our last sync:** April 5, 2026 (User Journeys deck)

---

## How to read this doc

Two weeks of build work, plus three pieces of competitive research you haven't seen yet. I'll walk you through where we are, what the research taught us, and the strategic decisions I'd like your input on before we plan the next deep passes. Everything here connects back to the core thesis: **community builds trust, trust enables care**.

If you only have 10 minutes, read sections **1, 2, and 5** (decisions). If you want full context, read top-to-bottom.

## 1. Where we are

The prototype has the full product skeleton. Every surface renders with real content. A tester can sign up, browse their community, find a meet, RSVP, review attendees after, connect with someone they met, see that person's profile, book care from them, and chat through the booking. None of this is faked with placeholders — the mock world has 20 users, 24 meets, 13 reviews, 10 bookings, 8 conversations, and 60+ generated images.

What's **not** yet real: meet creation end-to-end, guided demo / persona switching, session report cards, and the landing page rewrite for the community-first thesis.

We've shipped 30+ phases in total. Right now we're working through named **Deep Passes** — whole-surface quality rebuilds, one major area at a time, instead of incremental ticks. The goal is a world that feels real enough that a tester forgets it's a prototype.

## 2. What's changed since April 5

The highlights, in plain terms:

### Architecture
- **Panel architecture everywhere.** Home, Discover, Bookings, Schedule, Inbox all share one scrollable-panel pattern. Pages feel like one product, not five.
- **Profiles unified.** Every profile — owner, provider, other user, your own — uses the same layout with tabs: About · Posts · Services · Chat. The profile is now the relationship hub we always wanted it to be.
- **Bookings got real.** Tabbed detail page (Info · Sessions · Chat), rolling weekly billing, provider session actions (Start → Complete → Note). Owners and providers see the same booking from their different perspectives.
- **Post composer rebuilt** as a modal (desktop) / bottom sheet (mobile). Photo-first, tag accordion, minimal friction.

### Trust model — the big one this week
- **Post-meet review flow shipped** (`/meets/[id]/connect`). After a completed meet, an attendee sees a card stack of everyone who came and can mark each person as "Familiar" (one-sided, silent) or escalate to "Connect." Locked profiles show first-name-only with no dog reveal.
- **Schedule surfaces the prompt.** The "My Schedule" page now has a "Review recent meets" section (completed meets in the last 14 days) with a brand-accent Review CTA on the card.
- **New notification type** — post_meet_review — with two sample notifications in the notifications panel.

This is the **primary Familiar trigger** in the Trust & Connection Model. It was previously stubbed. Now it's the core mechanic for turning real-world meets into in-app trust signals, which in turn gate care services. This is the single most important feature for selling the community → trust → care story.

### Content depth
- 14 new generated profile images for our four personas + supporting cast. All mock users now have consistent, warm, professional headshots.
- Richer meet attendee lists with neighbourhood + breed metadata so the "people you know" social proof works.
- Trust signals on profiles (mutual connections, shared groups badges).
- Post attribution (in Group / at Place / with Dogs).

### Process shift
We moved from numbered phases (1, 2, 3...) to named Deep Passes (Meets Deep Pass, Discover & Care, Mock World Building). Each deep pass takes a whole surface and makes it the best it can be. Feels slower on paper but produces a more convincing product.

## 3. The vision (quick reset)

In one paragraph:

> **Doggo digitises how Prague dog owners actually build trust today** — through repeated informal meetings at parks, through neighbourhood regulars who become friends, through word-of-mouth recommendations. The app turns those loose patterns into a persistent social graph: meets create Familiar connections, Familiar deepens to Connected over time, and Connected unlocks care services. A trained walker who attends weekly meets in Letná earns trust badges organically by showing up — by the time you book them, you've already seen them handle dogs. The platform never replaces the relationship; it just makes it visible.

**Three Ways In** (all leading to the same network):
- **Find Your Park** — open groups at specific parks
- **Find Your People** — neighborhood and interest groups
- **Find Your Help** — care groups run by providers who also host community meets

**Four personas** we're building the world around:
- **Tereza** — routine owner, neighbourhood anchor. Morning Stromovka regular.
- **Daniel** — anxious new owner with a rescue. Lurks before joining.
- **Klára** — professional trainer. Uses meets as a client-acquisition channel.
- **Tomáš** — busy professional. Needs care more than community.

## 4. Competitive research — three studies

I did focused competitive research to pressure-test Doggo's thesis and look for features worth borrowing.

### Fluv — "Airbnb for pets" (Taiwan / Japan / HK)

**What they are:** A vetted marketplace for pet sitters and walkers. ~190K users, 9K sitters, ~$100K+ revenue in 2022 growing 3x in 2023.

**How they build trust:** Top-down vetting. Screening tests, ID verification, background checks, Japan Pet Sitter Association certification, platform-backed insurance, 3-day compensation window, **free in-home meet-and-greet before first booking**, and professional photography of sitters and their homes (borrowed from Airbnb).

**What we learn:**
- Fluv proves trust is the core problem in pet care — their entire pitch is "vetted, reliable, safe."
- Their approach gives **instant confidence** to a new user who's never met the sitter. Doggo's community approach builds deeper trust but takes time.
- Fluv has **no community layer.** Transactional: find sitter → book → done. Owners don't know each other. This is where Doggo differentiates — but Fluv's instant-confidence mechanism is worth stealing for the direct-discovery path (see section 5).
- **Social mission framing:** Fluv's "100K jobs for pet-loving women in Asia" is press-friendly and attracts sitters. Doggo's equivalent could be "turn your love for dogs into flexible income" — warmer than "become a provider."
- **Pet-data-based matching:** They use breed, size, temperament, special needs to match owners to sitters. Doggo has all this data already — filter only on service type and location today.

### Time To Pet — B2B pet care SaaS (US)

**What they are:** Back-office software for existing pet care businesses. 4K+ customers. Not a competitor — they assume you already have clients.

**What we learn:**
- **Visit Report Cards** after every session (photos + notes + GPS track + timestamps, delivered via push/email/SMS) are table stakes for pet parents. This is what makes owners feel safe and delighted.
- **Real-time walk updates** (live GPS, mid-walk photos) are trust accelerators, especially for anxious owners (Daniel).
- **Provider mobile workflow** is a focused in-session mode: pet info, quick actions, photo upload, notes. Not the full booking detail page.
- Doggo's rolling weekly billing is **already aligned** with their pattern — good validation.

### Prague dog care scene — local market study

**How trust works in Prague today, by tier:**

- **Tier 1 — Professional services** (Pawz, Dogitory, Rocky & Skipper). Trust through credentials (Czech cynology degrees, Národní kvalifikace Level 4), years of operation, named team members with bios, methodology declarations ("force-free, positive reinforcement"), and — universally — **daily photo/video/GPS updates** during sessions.
- **Tier 2 — Platform sitters** (Hlídačky.cz — 689 Prague sitters, absorbed DogInni). Trust through **HlídačkyVerify™** video ID check, "booked repeatedly" badges, "first aid trained" badges, language badges (English/Czech for the expat market), transparent hourly rates.
- **Tier 3 — Informal / word-of-mouth.** Facebook groups ("Pet sitting, dog walking Prague"), Expats.cz forums. Pure social proof: someone in the group vouches for a person. **This is the tier Doggo digitises.**

**Proposed badge system for Doggo provider profiles** (three tiers, all earned or declared):
- **Community-earned** — Community Regular, Neighbourhood Anchor, Trusted by Your Network, Repeat Clients, Care Veteran
- **Credential** — Certified Trainer, First Aid, Vet Background, Force-Free, Years Experience, Insured (self-declared with optional verification)
- **Platform** — Verified Identity, Responsive, Consistent (auto-calculated)

**Cold-start strategy:** Seed 3–5 providers in Vinohrady / Letná / Vršovice who use meets as a client-acquisition channel. Every meet they host generates real trust badges. Owners get value before they need care; providers get a client pipeline. This is the loop the prototype is selling.

## 5. Strategic considerations — decisions I'd like your input on

Six big ones, ranked by how much they'll shape the next deep passes.

### 5.1 Hybrid trust model

**The question:** Does Doggo offer any **top-down** trust signals (Verified ID, platform badges) alongside community-built trust, or stay pure community?

**Why it matters:** Fluv proves users want at least some confidence before committing. Prague platform sitters already use ID verification (HlídačkyVerify™). An anxious owner like Daniel may not attend three meets before booking a walker — he needs something faster. The risk of staying pure community is that the direct-discovery path (find a provider in Discover without prior meets) feels too cold.

**How it could work:** The three-tier badge system above. Community-earned badges are the **primary** trust signal (the Doggo moat), but credential and platform badges provide bridging confidence. A provider who's a Community Regular (10 meets attended) + Certified Trainer + Verified ID + Trusted by Your Network (3 of your connections use them) should feel as trustworthy as a Fluv sitter — even without a personal relationship.

**Sub-questions for you:**
- Which badges matter most for MVP? The full taxonomy needs prioritising.
- Does "Trusted by your network" require Connected status, or does Familiar count?
- Self-declared credentials only at launch, or verify any of them?
- Do we start with Verified ID as a platform signal, or defer to production?

**My recommendation:** Hybrid. Community trust primary, credential + lightweight platform badges as bridging signals. Scope to Discover & Care Deep Pass.

---

### 5.2 Intro session / pre-booking meet-and-greet

**The question:** Do we add a new "Intro Session" booking type — free or reduced price — for a first booking with a new provider?

**Why it matters:** Fluv offers a free in-home meet-and-greet before the first booking — no commitment. This is essentially what Doggo's community meets do organically for people who attend events. But for the **direct-discovery path** (Daniel finds Klára in Discover without prior meets), there's a trust gap. An Intro Session bridges it without forcing the long community path.

**How it could work:** When an owner books a provider they have no prior meets with, the provider can offer (or the platform suggests) an Intro Session first — a 30-minute walk or meet at a public park, free or reduced price. After the intro, the Post-Meet Review flow fires and the Familiar connection forms. Then the regular booking proceeds with trust already in place.

**Sub-questions:**
- Provider-optional or platform-required before first booking with unknown providers?
- Should Care groups require services listed first? (Related — care group meets already serve as intros.)

**My recommendation:** Add Intro Session as a booking type, provider-toggleable. Scope to Bookings Deep Pass.

---

### 5.3 Session experience — visit report cards and real-time updates

**The question:** How far do we go on the in-session / post-session experience?

**Why it matters:** Time To Pet and Prague professionals both treat **daily photo/note/GPS updates** as table stakes. Today, Doggo's "Complete session" is a text note. That's an obvious gap for a product competing for care trust.

**How it could work (three progressive tiers):**
1. **Visit Report Card** — provider completes session with photos + notes + timestamps (+ optional GPS summary). Delivered to owner as an in-app notification card and an email. In scope.
2. **Mid-session photo update** — provider can send a photo during an active session, owner sees in real-time. In scope, uses same infrastructure as #1.
3. **Live GPS during active sessions** — owner sees walker's live location on a map. Out of scope for prototype (needs real-time infrastructure). Note as future differentiator.

**Sub-questions:**
- How prominent does the "in-session" provider view need to be? Focused mode separate from booking detail, or overlay?
- What pet info surfaces in that provider view? (Meds, vet contact, behavioural notes, emergency info.)

**My recommendation:** Ship tiers 1 and 2 in Schedule & Bookings Deep Pass. Defer tier 3.

---

### 5.4 Post-meet review timing & the "aha moment"

**The question:** When does the post-meet review prompt fire, and what's the moment that makes a user come back?

**Why it matters:** The post-meet review (just shipped) is the primary Familiar trigger. Its timing shapes how often it fires and how many Familiars accumulate per meet. The "aha moment" question is broader — what's the first experience that makes a new user think "oh, this is actually useful"?

**Options for timing:**
- **Immediately after the meet ends** — warmer memory, but people are still at the park or on their way home
- **Next-morning push** — lower urgency but better context, they're on their phone with coffee
- **Card in Schedule** only (no push) — respects the attention economy but slower trust accumulation

**My recommendation:** Next-morning push + permanent Schedule card ("Review recent meets" section already shipped). Test both push and no-push variants in demo.

**Aha moment candidates to discuss:**
- Walking into a park group for the first time and seeing people you recognise
- The first Familiar → Connected moment (someone you marked familiar has also marked you)
- Seeing your neighbourhood feed populated by people from this morning's walk

---

### 5.5 Demo packaging

**The question:** How do we present the prototype to testers?

**Options:**
- **Persona entry page** — "See what it's like as Tereza / Daniel / Klára / Tomáš" with pre-loaded state
- **Guided tour** — walks a tester through the community → trust → care funnel with annotations
- **Free exploration** — drop them into the app, let them wander
- **All three** — persona selection → optional guided tour → free exploration

**Sub-questions:**
- What 3–4 pages tell each persona's story best?
- Do we adopt an "Airbnb for pets"-shaped tagline, or own a more community-forward framing? ("Airbnb for pets" is immediately understood; "community for dog owners that also enables care" needs explaining.)

**My recommendation:** Persona entry → short guided highlight reel → free exploration. This is the last deep pass before we ship.

---

### 5.6 Cold-start seeding — post-prototype but worth aligning

**The question:** When do we start recruiting Prague providers, and what's the pitch?

**Why it matters:** The whole thesis rests on providers using meets as a client-acquisition channel. 3–5 seed providers in Vinohrady / Letná / Vršovice running weekly meets is enough to test the loop.

**Sub-questions:**
- Can we identify 3–5 candidate providers to approach?
- What's the ROI pitch? (How many weeks before meet attendees convert to clients?)
- Seed provider incentives — free period, reduced fee, featured placement?

**My recommendation:** Not for this deep pass arc. But worth starting a shortlist now — I can help draft outreach when you're ready.
